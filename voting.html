Voting

Voting is one way for groups of people to make choices. There are other ways for groups of people to make choices, such as by having a dictator who makes choices. In this post, I discuss voting systems. Some questions I am interested in:

  • How can groups of people make optimal decisions?
  • How can we make voting systems efficient?

Arrow's Impossibility Theorem

Arrow's impossibility theorem states that when voters have three or more options, there is no ranked voting system that satisfies the following properties:

Property Definition
Complete and transitive All options are ranked in order. To satisfy this property, there must be no circular ties, such as in rock-paper-scissors (rock beats scissors, scissors beats paper, paper beats rock)
Unrestricted domain All voters have a say
Non-dictatorship The decision is not left up to a single person, or in other words, there is no dictator
Pareto efficiency Nobody can be made better off without making someone else worse off
